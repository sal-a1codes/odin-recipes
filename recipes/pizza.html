Pizza

Description

A basic, homemade pizza can be made with simple pantry staples in about 1-2 hours (including rise time).

The key is high heat to get a crispy crust.

Ingredients

  • Warm Water: 1 cup (approx. 110°F/45°C)
  • Instant or Active Dry Yeast: 1 package (2 ¼ tsp)
  • Sugar: 1 tbsp (helps activate yeast)
  • Olive Oil: 1-2 tbsp, plus more for greasing
  • Salt: 1 tsp
  • All-Purpose Flour or Bread Flour: 2 ½ to 3 ½ cups
  • Toppings: 1/2 cup pizza sauce, 1-2 cups shredded mozzarella, and pepperoni/other toppings as desired

Steps

  1. Activate Yeast: Combine warm water, sugar, and yeast in a large bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Make Dough: Add olive oil, salt, and flour to the mixture. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead: Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 30-60 minutes, or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat & Shape: Preheat your oven to its highest setting—usually 450°F-500°F (230°C-260°C). Punch down the dough and stretch it into a 12-inch circle on a baking sheet or pizza pan.
  6. Top & Bake: Add sauce, cheese, and toppings. Bake for 10-15 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
